About

At the forefront of Earth and planetary science, our research group investigates the dynamic processes shaping the lithosphere and deep interior of the Earth. Our work spans a broad range of scales, from plate tectonics and mantle flow to crustal architecture and local stress evolution, with a special focus on integrating multi-disciplinary geophysical observations.

Research Focus

  • Lithospheric Structure & Seismic Imaging
    We use seismic techniques such as ambient noise tomography, receiver function analysis, H-κ stacking, and joint inversion to image crust and mantle structure. Our recent work includes thermal and compositional models of the U.S. crust and subduction zone studies in Alaska and East Antarctica.
  • Geodynamic Modeling
    We develop regional and global models of lithosphere–mantle coupling using inputs like GPE, strain rates, and tomography. These models help explain plate boundary deformation, intraplate seismicity, and landscape evolution.
  • Geothermal Heat Flow & Ice Sheet Dynamics
    We generate empirical geothermal heat flux maps and investigate crustal anisotropy beneath Antarctica, advancing both tectonic and climate research.
  • Earth System Interactions
    Our group explores how tectonic and surface processes interact with climate and biodiversity over geologic timescales— including studies linking tectonic extension and paleoelevation to mammalian diversification in western North America.
  • Method Development & Data Integration
    We develop Monte Carlo joint inversion frameworks and integrate seismic, geodetic, and geological datasets to better constrain Earth structure and deformation.

Collaborative & Interdisciplinary

Our research is deeply collaborative, involving partnerships across institutions and disciplines—spanning tectonics, seismology, geochronology, and geobiology. Whether modeling crustal strain in California or reconstructing paleotopography in the Basin and Range, we connect deep Earth processes to surface expression and long-term Earth system evolution.
